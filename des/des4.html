Hamer(ሀመር))

The Hamer are a renowned Tribe of Omo Valley, particularly for their Women’s Striking hairstyles, and friendliest nature among the people in Ethiopia. The people mainly live in the Hamer Land. Bordering Chew Bahir to the east, Kenya in the south, Bena Land in the north, and Omo river in the west. They are Neighboring Bena, Stemaya, Arbore, Karo, Dasenech, Karo, and Gnangatom Tribes. Today there are around 50,000 Hamer people. ......

According to prominent elders of the tribe, “Hamer” means “people who live between mountains and rocks, and a mixed group”. This ancient oral legend continues, the Hamer used to live between mountains and on a mountainous Place before they settle at present areas. The Hamer made themselves through a combination of different tribes.

Hamers live in a polygamous society. A Hamer man can have many wives. Arranged marriages are common with the family of the young girl choosing their daughter’s husband with a Dowery. Then the boys are expected to perform a rite of passage by jumping Bulls. This is considered a rite of passage to manhood. There are also Consensual Marriage, Abduction marriage, inheritance marriage when the older brother died, and replacement marriage.
